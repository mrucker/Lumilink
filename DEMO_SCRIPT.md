# LumiLink Advisory Board Demo Script
### Duration: ~10 Minutes

---

## OPENING (1 minute)

**[Start with the app open on the Garden View]**

"Thank you all for being here. Today we're walking you through LumiLink -- a relationship wellness app that helps people nurture and maintain their real-world connections.

The core idea is simple: relationships take work, and most people don't have a system for that. LumiLink gives them one -- with visual feedback, smart task management, memory journaling, and AI-powered suggestions to keep relationships growing.

Let me show you what we've built so far."

---

## SECTION 1: THE GARDEN -- VISUALIZING RELATIONSHIPS (1.5 minutes)

**[Stay on Garden View]**

"This is the Garden -- our default theme. Every flower here represents a real relationship in the user's life. The taller and fuller the flower, the stronger that relationship is.

Before I go further, I want to note that we're still in the prototyping phase. The focus right now has been on building out functionality and getting the core experience right. The graphics and visual polish are something we plan to refine as we move forward -- so what you're seeing today is about the ideas and interactions, not final design.

You'll notice two sections: Friends & Family up top, and Work Connections below. This separation helps users think about their relationships in context.

**[Point to a tall flower vs. a short flower]**

Each flower's height is driven by a relationship strength score from 0 to 100. That score changes based on how actively you engage -- completing tasks, logging memories, staying in touch.

**[Tap the 'Add New Relationship' button briefly]**

Adding a new connection is straightforward -- name, category, and a SkyLink ID to connect with them in-app."

---

## SECTION 2: THEMES -- CITY & DESERT (1 minute)

**[Tap the settings icon in the top-right corner]**

"One thing that makes LumiLink feel personal is our theme system. Let me switch to the City view."

**[Select City theme]**

"Same data, completely different feel. Now relationships are buildings in a skyline. Stronger relationships are taller buildings. We have a Friends & Family district and a Work District.

**[Switch to Desert theme]**

And here's the Desert Oasis. Relationships become desert plants -- cacti, palms, tropical flowers. Users can even pick which plant type represents each friend.

Each theme has its own independent color customization, so the app truly feels like yours. But under the hood, all three themes share the same data and functionality."

**[Switch back to Garden for the next section]**

---

## SECTION 3: FRIEND DETAIL VIEW & RELATIONSHIP CONTEXT (2 minutes)

**[Tap on a flower -- e.g., Sarah who has strong trend data]**

"When you tap a connection, you get their detail view. At the top: their name, a visual of their flower, and a relationship strength bar showing 85%.

Below the bar, there's a trend indicator -- in Sarah's case, a green upward arrow with '3 tasks completed this month.' This tells the user which direction things are heading and why. It turns a number into something they can feel and act on."

**[Tap a friend with a down trend, like Jake or Marcus, briefly to show contrast]**

"Now look at Marcus -- a red downward arrow saying 'No activity in 2 weeks.' That's a gentle nudge to reconnect. The trend gives every relationship context without overwhelming the user."

**[Go back to Sarah's detail view]**

"Below that we have four tabs:

**[Tap through each tab briefly]**

- **Tasks** -- things you've planned to do with or for this person
- **Photos** -- shared photo memories
- **Bucket List** -- long-term dreams and goals you share
- **About** -- relationship metadata: how you met, when you became friends, shared interests, communication preferences

**[Scroll down to show AI Recommendations]**

Down here, LumiLink uses Claude AI to generate personalized activity suggestions. The AI considers relationship strength, shared interests, communication style, and what you've already done together. So instead of generic advice, it gives specific, actionable ideas.

**[Tap one of the AI suggestions]**

With one tap, that suggestion becomes a task -- pre-filled and ready to schedule."

---

## SECTION 4: TASK MANAGEMENT (2 minutes)

**[Navigate to the Tasks tab via bottom nav]**

"Tasks are the heartbeat of LumiLink. This is where intentions become actions.

**[Show the main task list]**

Tasks are organized by date with urgency indicators -- urgent, soon, or upcoming. You can see completion stats at the top: how many tasks total, your completion rate.

**[Tap to create a new task]**

Creating a task is simple -- give it a title, pick which friend or friends it involves, and optionally set a date.

**[Toggle the Group Activity switch]**

Here's a key feature: Group Activities. You can create a single task that involves multiple friends -- like a group dinner or a weekend trip. You name the group, pick your people, and everyone's on the same task.

**[Close the creation modal]**

**[Show the Requests tab]**

We also have a task request system. Friends can send you activity requests, and you can accept or decline. This makes relationship maintenance a two-way street.

**[Complete a task by tapping the checkbox]**

Now watch what happens when I complete a task. LumiLink prompts you for a reflection -- how did it go? You can optionally add a photo. This reflection automatically becomes a memory entry, so your experiences are captured without extra effort."

---

## SECTION 5: MEMORIES (1 minute)

**[Navigate to the Memories tab via bottom nav]**

"This is the Memories view -- a calendar-based journal of your relationship moments.

**[Show dots on calendar dates indicating memories]**

Dates with memories are highlighted. Let me tap on one.

**[Tap a date with memories]**

Each memory has a photo, a caption, a location, and tags showing which friends were there. These are created automatically from task reflections, or users can add them manually.

**[Tap a photo to expand it]**

This creates a rich, visual history of your relationships over time. Three months from now, you can look back and see exactly what you did, who you were with, and how it felt."

---

## SECTION 6: PROFILE & CUSTOMIZATION (1 minute)

**[Navigate to Profile via bottom nav]**

"The Profile screen is the user's dashboard.

**[Point out the stats section]**

At the top: total connections, new connections this month, active tasks, and weekly completion progress with improvement tracking.

**[Show wellness tracking]**

We track wellness across three categories -- Mental, Physical, and Social -- giving users a holistic view of how their relationship habits affect their wellbeing.

**[Scroll to customization options]**

Users can customize their avatar -- gender, hairstyle, shirt color -- and set independent color schemes for each theme.

**[Scroll to Privacy & Safety]**

And importantly, we've built in privacy controls from day one: location privacy, connection range limits, a block list, and verification status."

---

## CLOSING (30 seconds)

"To summarize what we've built:

- **Three fully themed visual environments** for relationship tracking
- **Trend-based nudges** that give relationship strength real context
- **A complete task management system** with individual tasks, group activities, and friend-to-friend requests
- **AI-powered recommendations** personalized to each relationship
- **A memory journal** that captures experiences automatically
- **Profile customization, statistics, wellness tracking, and privacy controls**

Again, we're in the prototyping phase -- the core functionality is in place, and visual polish is next on our roadmap. We'd love your feedback -- what resonates, what's missing, and where we should focus next. Thank you."

## TIPS FOR THE PRESENTER

- Keep the app on a phone or phone-sized simulator for the most authentic feel (designed for iPhone 14 Pro, 390x844px)
- Have the Express backend running (`node server.js`) so AI recommendations load live
- Pre-populate a friend with rich relationship metadata so the AI demo is compelling
- Practice the task completion -> reflection -> memory flow so it's smooth
- Show at least one "up trend" friend (Sarah) and one "down trend" friend (Marcus) to demonstrate the nudge contrast
- If AI recommendations take a moment to load, use that pause to explain what's happening behind the scenes
